VII. Lessons and Impact

For the Early Church

  • Shift from primarily Jewish to multi-ethnic church, fulfilling God's promise to Abraham that "in thee shall all families of the earth be blessed" (Genesis 12:3)
  • Leadership multiplication through elder appointment, establishing the pattern described in Titus 1:5: "ordain elders in every city"
  • Encouraged other missionary efforts, setting a precedent for the Great Commission (Matthew 28:19-20)
  • Demonstrated the Holy Spirit's active guidance in mission work: "So they, being sent forth by the Holy Ghost, departed unto Seleucia" (Acts 13:4)

For Believers Today

  • Availability of salvation to all nations: "There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither bond nor free, there is neither male nor female: for ye are all one in Christ Jesus" (Galatians 3:28)
  • Importance of courage, discernment, and faithfulness in mission: "Be strong in the Lord, and in the power of his might" (Ephesians 6:10)
  • Model of church planting, discipleship, and leadership development: "And the things that thou hast heard of me among many witnesses, the same commit thou to faithful men, who shall be able to teach others also" (2 Timothy 2:2)
  • Willingness to suffer for Gospel advancement: "For unto you it is given in the behalf of Christ, not only to believe on him, but also to suffer for his sake" (Philippians 1:29)
