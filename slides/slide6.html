IV. Key Events: Cyprus

  • "And when they were at Salamis, they preached the word of God in the synagogues of the Jews" (Acts 13:5)
  • At Paphos, met Sergius Paulus, Roman proconsul—described as "a prudent man" (Acts 13:7)
  • Archaeological evidence: Inscriptions bearing the name "Sergius Paulus" have been discovered on Cyprus, confirming Luke's historical accuracy
  • Conflict with Bar-Jesus/Elymas the sorcerer:
    • "O full of all subtilty and all mischief, thou child of the devil, thou enemy of all righteousness, wilt thou not cease to pervert the right ways of the Lord?" (Acts 13:10)
    • "And now, behold, the hand of the Lord is upon thee, and thou shalt be blind, not seeing the sun for a season" (Acts 13:11)
  • Sergius Paulus believed, "being astonished at the doctrine of the Lord" (Acts 13:12)—first recorded high-ranking Roman official convert

Significance:

Demonstrates spiritual warfare and Paul's emergence as team leader (shift from "Barnabas and Saul" in Acts 13:7 to "Paul and his company" in Acts 13:13). Cyprus was strategically important as Barnabas's homeland (Acts 4:36), providing a natural starting point with established connections.
