IV. Key Events: Lystra

Lystra

  • Paul heals a man crippled from birth who "never had walked" (Acts 14:8)
  • Locals believe Paul is Mercurius and Barnabas is Jupiter: "The gods are come down to us in the likeness of men" (Acts 14:11)
  • Cultural context: Local legend (recorded by Ovid) told of Jupiter and Mercurius visiting the region in human form and being rejected by all except one elderly couple
  • "Then the priest of Jupiter, which was before their city, brought oxen and garlands unto the gates, and would have done sacrifice with the people" (Acts 14:13)
  • Paul and Barnabas object: "Sirs, why do ye these things? We also are men of like passions with you" (Acts 14:15)
  • First recorded sermon to pagans focuses on creation and natural revelation rather than Jewish history
  • Dramatic shift from adoration to stoning Paul: "And there came thither certain Jews from Antioch and Iconium, who persuaded the people, and, having stoned Paul, drew him out of the city, supposing he had been dead" (Acts 14:19)
  • Paul miraculously recovers: "Howbeit, as the disciples stood round about him, he rose up, and came into the city" (Acts 14:20)
  • Timothy's hometown: Paul likely met Timothy's family here (Acts 16:1-3)

First sermon to a purely pagan audience: "That ye should turn from these vanities unto the living God, which made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and all things that are therein" (Acts 14:15)
