VI. Theological Significance (Continued)

Justification by Faith

Emphasizes faith over the Law for salvation: "And by him all that believe are justified from all things, from which ye could not be justified by the law of Moses" (Acts 13:39), anticipating Paul's fuller development in Romans and Galatians

Persecution as Normative

The journey is marked by opposition and stoning, but also fruitfulness, confirming Jesus's warning: "In the world ye shall have tribulation: but be of good cheer; I have overcome the world" (John 16:33) and Paul's later teaching that "all that will live godly in Christ Jesus shall suffer persecution" (2 Timothy 3:12)
