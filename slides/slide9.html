IV. Key Events: Iconium

Iconium

  • Spoke in the synagogue; "a great multitude both of the Jews and also of the Greeks believed" (Acts 14:1)
  • "But the unbelieving Jews stirred up the Gentiles, and made their minds evil affected against the brethren" (Acts 14:2)
  • "Long time therefore abode they speaking boldly in the Lord" (Acts 14:3)
  • Paul and Barnabas perform miraculous signs and wonders: "the Lord... gave testimony unto the word of his grace, and granted signs and wonders to be done by their hands" (Acts 14:3)
  • "But the multitude of the city was divided: and part held with the Jews, and part with the apostles" (Acts 14:4)
  • Attempted stoning, so "they were ware of it, and fled unto Lystra and Derbe" (Acts 14:6)
  • Historical note: Iconium (modern Konya, Turkey) was a major Phrygian city at the intersection of important trade routes
