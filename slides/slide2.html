I. Background and Context

Paul the Apostle

  • Formerly Saul of Tarsus, a devout Pharisee (Philippians 3:5)
  • Persecuted Christians before his conversion (Acts 8:1-3; 9:1-2; Galatians 1:13)
  • Encountered the risen Christ on the Damascus Road (Acts 9:3-9)
  • Called specifically to the Gentile mission: "But the Lord said unto him, Go thy way: for he is a chosen vessel unto me, to bear my name before the Gentiles, and kings, and the children of Israel" (Acts 9:15)
  • Spent formative years in Arabia and Damascus before ministry (Galatians 1:17-18)

The Church at Antioch

  • Major hub for Christian ministry after persecution in Jerusalem (Acts 11:19-21)
  • First place believers were called "Christians" (Acts 11:26)
  • Multi-ethnic leadership: "Now there were in the church that was at Antioch certain prophets and teachers; as Barnabas, and Simeon that was called Niger, and Lucius of Cyrene, and Manaen, which had been brought up with Herod the tetrarch, and Saul" (Acts 13:1)
  • Demonstrated practical love by sending relief to Judean believers during famine (Acts 11:27-30)
  • Located in Syria, Antioch was the third-largest city in the Roman Empire after Rome and Alexandria, with an estimated population of 500,000
